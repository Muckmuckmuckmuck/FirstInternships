## 1. Snapshot

- **Name:** FirstInternships
- **Wordmark in product:** `firstinternships` (lowercase)
- **URL:** firstinternships.com
- **Category:** AI-powered internship outreach platform (SaaS web app)
- **One-liner:** *FirstInternships helps students land internships by reaching out directly — it gives you a curated database of company recruiting inboxes, writes a personalized cold email for each one with AI, and sends it from your own Gmail.*
- **Elevator pitch:** Most students fire off applications into job boards and never hear back. The truth is companies hire students who reach out directly — but cold emailing hundreds of firms is slow, awkward, and easy to get wrong. FirstInternships fixes that: browse a curated database of real recruiting inboxes (or let AI discover more), get an email personalized to each company and your background, send it from your own inbox in your voice, and track every reply, interview, and offer in one pipeline.

---

## 2. What we do (in detail)

FirstInternships turns the internship search from "apply and wait" into "reach out and track." The product does five things:

1. **Curated contact database.** Thousands of company recruiting inboxes (`careers@`, `jobs@`, recruiting/talent addresses) across dozens of industries — finance, asset management, private equity, venture capital, consulting, tech, and more.
2. **AI personalization.** For any company, the AI writes a cold email tailored to what that company actually does and to the student's real background and résumé — not a template.
3. **Send from your own Gmail.** Connect Gmail once (OAuth, no API keys, no copy-paste). Emails go out from the student's own address, in their own voice.
4. **Pipeline & reply tracking.** Every contact moves through a status pipeline: Contacted → Replied → Interview → Offer. Save companies to target lists (Dream / Reach / Realistic).
5. **Follow-ups & deliverability protection.** Reminders to follow up (where most replies come from), plus guardrails that keep a student's personal inbox from getting spam-flagged when sending at volume.

## 3. How it works (the user flow — great for "how it works" ads)

1. **Sign up** and add a short profile + résumé.
2. **Connect Gmail** (one click).
3. **Browse or search** the firm database — or use **AI discovery** (Pro) to find companies beyond it.
4. **Pick a company** → the AI **writes a personalized email** referencing that company's work and your background.
5. **Send from your Gmail.** It's unlocked; every future email/follow-up to them is free.
6. **Track the reply** → mark Replied / Interview / Offer, get follow-up nudges.
7. **Repeat** — build momentum across a list of target companies.

## 4. Who it's for

**Primary audience:** College students (and recent grads) hunting for internships in **competitive, relationship-driven fields** — finance, asset management, private equity, venture capital, consulting, and tech — where the best roles are never posted and direct outreach wins.

**Also:** any ambitious student who's tired of applying into the void and wants a faster, more direct path.

**Personas:**
- **"The finance hopeful"** — sophomore/junior targeting PE/VC/AM/IB internships, knows networking matters but doesn't know how to start or what to say. Competitive, driven, time-poor.
- **"The ghosted applicant"** — has applied to 50+ postings on Handshake/LinkedIn and heard nothing. Frustrated, looking for something that actually works.
- **"The go-getter underdog"** — no fancy network or target-school pedigree; willing to hustle via cold outreach if given the tools.

**Emotional drivers:** ambition, urgency (internship season), fear of falling behind peers, wanting an edge, wanting to feel in control of the process.

## 11. Brand voice & tone

**Personality:** Direct, confident, motivating, a little bold. Talks to students like a sharp older friend who's been through it — not a corporate brand, not a hype-bro.

**Principles:**
- **Direct over clever.** Say the thing. Short sentences.
- **Confident, not arrogant.** We back the student; we don't trash-talk them for "doing it wrong."
- **Action-oriented.** Verbs: reach out, send, track, land, move.
- **Honest.** No inflated promises. (See §13.)
- **Aspirational but grounded.** Big outcome, real mechanism.

**Tone do's:**
- "Stop applying into the void."
- "The job isn't on the job board."
- "Reach out. Get replies. Land the internship."

**Tone don'ts:**
- ❌ Corporate fluff ("synergize your career journey")
- ❌ Fake urgency / scammy hype ("GET HIRED OVERNIGHT!!!")
- ❌ Talking down to students or implying they're failures
- ❌ Over-promising guaranteed jobs

## 13. Honesty guardrails (what we DON'T claim — IMPORTANT)

We hold a high bar on honesty. Ads must **not**:
- ❌ Call contacts **"verified"** — they're **curated company recruiting inboxes**, not deliverability-verified. Say "curated," "real," or "recruiting inboxes."
- ❌ Claim we're the **"fastest"** or work for **"every"** industry — use "a direct path" and "dozens of industries."
- ❌ **Guarantee** a job, interview, or reply.
- ❌ Imply we send spam or automate mass-blasting — we *protect* deliverability and send personal emails.

Safe, true proof points: thousands of curated contacts across dozens of industries; AI personalization grounded in real company info + your résumé; send from your own Gmail; track replies/interviews/offers; built-in deliverability guardrails.

We run **two coordinated looks**. Use the one that fits the placement.

### A) Product / "clean" look (light) — Apollo.io / Linear / Vercel cousin
White backgrounds, near-black text, a single blue accent, hairline borders, generous whitespace. Sharp, editorial, premium-minimal. Use for product screenshots, app-UI ads, carousels, anything showing the tool.

Near-black backgrounds, glowing blue accent, white type, data-viz motifs (node fields, tables, pipelines, an email writing itself, replies cascading in). Premium, confident, a little dramatic. Use for hero videos, brand ads, big statements.

### Typography
- **Product / UI & clean ads: Inter.** Weights 400/500/600/700/800. Big headings use **tight negative letter-spacing** (about −0.5 to −1.5px) and heavy weight (700–800). Body 400–500. Email addresses shown in **monospace** for a "real inbox" feel.
- **Cinematic / video: Poppins** (Bold / Medium / Light) for large on-screen statements. *Note: Poppins lacks some glyphs (→ ✓ ✦ ✨) — substitute `»` and `•` in rendered type to avoid broken boxes.*
- **Hierarchy feel:** one bold statement, lots of breathing room, minimal secondary text.

### Logo / wordmark
- The identity is the **wordmark "FirstInternships"** (in-app it appears lowercase as `firstinternships`). No ornate logo mark — keep it typographic.
- Recommended treatment: bold Inter (or Poppins for cinematic) wordmark, near-black on white or white on near-black, with the blue accent used sparingly (e.g., on a period, underline, or single letter). Keep it clean and confident.

### Aesthetic / mood
- **Editorial, sharp, minimal, premium.** Think Linear / Vercel / Apollo.io (light) and Palantir (dark).
- Mostly **flat color** (no decorative gradients), **hairline borders**, lots of **whitespace**, **single accent** discipline (blue) over a monochrome base.
- **Motifs:** the inbox / email, a directory or table of companies, a status pipeline, a node-field/data-viz of companies lighting up when "matched," an email composing itself, replies arriving (green).
- **Feel to evoke:** control, momentum, a quiet edge — "serious tool for ambitious people," not "cutesy student app."

### Imagery & iconography
- Clean line icons, thin strokes, monochrome with blue accent.
- Real-feeling UI (inbox, composer, pipeline) over stock photography. If using people, show focused, ambitious students / a single founder-energy protagonist — not cheesy corporate stock.
- Avoid clutter, drop shadows everywhere, and rainbow palettes. One accent, lots of space.
